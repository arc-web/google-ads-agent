# Wright's Impact Window and Door Regional Strategy

## Overview

This regional strategy document provides comprehensive geographic targeting for Wright's Impact Window and Door Google Ads campaigns. The focus is exclusively on **Lee County** (West Coast) and **Broward County** (East Coast), creating a targeted coastal corridor strategy that leverages the company's dual office presence in Punta Gorda (Lee County area) and West Palm Beach (adjacent to Broward County).

**Key Principles:**
- Zero geographic gaps through complete ZIP code coverage
- County-level overreach for comprehensive market coverage
- City prioritization based on population, income, and storm risk
- ZIP code prioritization aligned with city priorities
- Strategic exclusion of non-target areas

## Geographic Scope

### Target Counties
- **Lee County, FL** (West Coast - Primary Office Region)
- **Broward County, FL** (East Coast - Primary Market Region)

### Geographic Strategy
- **No radius targeting** - All targeting at county, city, and ZIP code level
- **No DMA zoning** - Explicit geographic boundaries only
- **Complete coverage** - Every ZIP code within target cities included
- **Coastal corridor** - Gulf Coast to Atlantic Coast coverage via two strategic counties

## NEGATIVE REGIONS (Exclusion Strategy)

- **Broward County**: 33004, 33008, 33009, 33012, 33013 (economic distress areas)

### Geographic Exclusion Zones
**Areas Outside Service Capability:**
- **Lee County**: Rural southern areas (33944-33947) - limited service infrastructure
- **Broward County**: Extreme northern areas (33345-33349) - outside primary service radius from West Palm Beach office

### Competitive Exclusion Zones
**Oversaturated Markets:**
- **Lee County**: 34102 (extreme luxury market, limited ROI potential)
- **Broward County**: 33301 (ultra-luxury downtown, high competition)

### Environmental Exclusion Zones
**Flood-Prone/High-Risk Areas:**
- **Lee County**: Barrier island ZIP codes during storm season (33931, 33957)
- **Broward County**: Coastal flood zones (33304, 33305, 33306) during active hurricane season

## PERFORMANCE MONITORING

### Geographic KPIs to Track
- **Impression Share by County**: Target >70% in primary markets
- **Cost per Acquisition by ZIP Code**: Monitor performance variance
- **Conversion Rate by City Priority**: Top priority cities should outperform secondary
- **Geographic Performance Trends**: Seasonal and economic impact analysis

### Geographic Optimization Triggers
- **ZIP Code Performance**: Reallocate budget from underperforming to high-performing ZIP codes
- **City-Level Analysis**: Regular review of top vs. secondary priority performance
- **County-Level Balance**: Maintain 60/40 split between Lee and Broward counties
- **Exclusion Review**: Quarterly assessment of negative region effectiveness

### Geographic Reporting Structure
```
Geographic Report:
├── Lee County Performance
```
