## PRIMARY BUDGET ALLOCATION

### Overall Geographic Split
- **Lee County (West Coast)**: 60% of total budget
- **Broward County (East Coast)**: 40% of total budget

**Rationale**: Higher allocation to Lee County reflects larger service area, established presence with Punta Gorda office, and higher hurricane vulnerability requiring more proactive marketing.

### Lee County Budget Distribution

#### TOP PRIORITY CITIES ALLOCATION
- **Fort Myers**: 40% of Lee County budget ($240 daily at $600 total)
  - **Rationale**: Primary business hub, highest population density, major commercial center
  - **Opportunity**: Large residential and commercial markets, growing economy

- **Cape Coral**: 25% of Lee County budget ($150 daily at $600 total)
  - **Rationale**: High-growth residential area, expanding population, new construction
  - **Opportunity**: Affluent retirement community, property improvement focus

- **Naples**: 20% of Lee County budget ($120 daily at $600 total)
  - **Rationale**: Luxury market, high-value properties, premium service positioning
  - **Opportunity**: Upscale residential market, property value enhancement

#### SECONDARY CITIES ALLOCATION
- **Bonita Springs, Estero, Lehigh Acres, Sanibel/Captiva**: 15% of Lee County budget ($90 daily at $600 total)
  - **Rationale**: Growth areas with emerging opportunity, supplemental coverage
  - **Opportunity**: Population growth, vacation home market

### Broward County Budget Distribution

#### TOP PRIORITY CITIES ALLOCATION
- **Fort Lauderdale**: 35% of Broward County budget ($140 daily at $400 total)
  - **Rationale**: Downtown business district, highest commercial concentration
  - **Opportunity**: Urban professional market, commercial property focus

- **Hollywood**: 25% of Broward County budget ($100 daily at $400 total)
  - **Rationale**: Beach communities, tourist destination, coastal properties
  - **Opportunity**: Vacation rental market, storm protection demand

- **Pembroke Pines**: 20% of Broward County budget ($80 daily at $400 total)
  - **Rationale**: Family communities, suburban growth, established neighborhoods
  - **Opportunity**: Family home improvements, community associations

#### SECONDARY CITIES ALLOCATION
- **Coral Springs, Sunrise, Plantation, Davie, Miramar, Tamarac, North Lauderdale, Lauderhill, Oakland Park**: 20% of Broward County budget ($80 daily at $400 total)
  - **Rationale**: Established residential areas, diverse demographics
  - **Opportunity**: Mature neighborhoods, renovation market

## PERFORMANCE-BASED BUDGET OPTIMIZATION

### Performance-Based Reallocation Triggers

#### High-Performance Cities (ROI >400%)
- **Automatic Increase**: +15% budget allocation
- **Cap**: Maximum 50% of county budget
- Performance monitoring until improvement trends stabilize

#### Underperforming Cities (ROI <250%)
- **Floor**: Minimum 5% of county budget
- **Review Period**: 90 days before permanent reduction

#### New Market Testing
- **Test Budget**: 5% of county allocation for new ZIP codes
- **Success Criteria**: Positive ROI within 30 days

### Geographic Performance Metrics

#### Primary KPIs for Budget Decisions
- **Cost per Acquisition**: Target <$100 (industry average $150)
- **Return on Ad Spend**: Target >400% (industry standard 300%)
- **Conversion Rate**: Target >4% (phone calls + form submissions)
- **Geographic Share of Voice**: Target >70% in primary markets

#### Secondary Performance Indicators
- **Impression Share**: Monitor competitive positioning
- **Click-Through Rate**: Quality score and ad relevance
- **Cost per Click**: Efficiency of keyword targeting
- **Customer Lifetime Value**: Long-term ROI measurement
