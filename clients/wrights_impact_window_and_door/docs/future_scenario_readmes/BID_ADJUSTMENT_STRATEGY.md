
# BID ADJUSTMENT STRATEGY - Wright's Impact Window and Door

## Purpose
Document device, audience, and geographic bid adjustment approaches for Wright's local service business optimization.

## Core Strategy
As a local service business, Wright's requires mobile-first optimization with geographic precision and audience targeting.

## Device Bid Adjustments

### Mobile Bid Modifier (+40%)
**When to Use**: All campaigns (primary traffic source)
**Rationale**: 70%+ of "near me" searches occur on mobile devices
**Implementation**: 
- Set mobile bid modifier to +40%
- Monitor mobile conversion rate vs. desktop
- Adjust based on mobile CPA performance

### Tablet Bid Modifier (+10%)  
**When to Use**: Performance Max campaigns
**Rationale**: Tablet users often in research phase
**Implementation**:
- Set tablet modifier to +10%
- Lower priority than mobile but higher than desktop

### Desktop Bid Modifier (-10%)
**When to Use**: When mobile performance justifies reduction
**Rationale**: Desktop users less likely to convert for local services
**Implementation**:
- Start at 0%, reduce to -10% if mobile CPA 30% better

## Geographic Bid Adjustments

### High-Value ZIP Code Boost (+25%)
**When to Use**: Premium neighborhoods in Lee/Broward counties
**ZIP Codes**: Naples 34102-34108, Fort Myers elite areas
**Rationale**: High-income homeowners with hurricane risk
**Implementation**:
- Target specific high-ROI ZIP codes
- Monitor geographic performance reports
- Adjust based on cost per lead by area

### Service Area Boundary (-20%)
**When to Use**: ZIP codes at edge of service radius
**Rationale**: Reduce wasted spend on distant inquiries
**Implementation**:
- Identify ZIP codes >25 miles from offices
- Apply -20% modifier to reduce traffic

## Audience Bid Adjustments

### Homeowner Audience (+30%)
**When to Use**: Impact window/door campaigns
**Rationale**: Homeowners are primary target market
**Implementation**:
- Target Google audience segments for homeowners
- Monitor audience performance overlap
- Increase bid for high-intent homeowner searches

### Recent Home Buyers (+50%)
**When to Use**: New construction areas
**Rationale**: New homeowners need impact protection immediately
**Implementation**:
- Target "recent home buyers" audience segment
- Combine with new construction ZIP codes
- Highest bid modifier for immediate conversion potential

### Income Targeting (+15% for $100K+)
**When to Use**: Premium service campaigns
**Rationale**: Higher-income households can afford full installations
**Implementation**:
- Target $100K+ household income segments
- Monitor conversion value vs. cost
- Adjust based on customer lifetime value

## Seasonal Bid Adjustments

### **When to Use**:  30 (**Rationale**: Peak demand period for impact protection
**Implementation**:
- Increase all modifiers by additional +20%
- Focus on storm preparation messaging
- Monitor insurance-driven conversions

### **When to Use**:  31 (**Rationale**: Reduced demand for non-emergency installations
**Implementation**:
- Reduce base modifiers by 30%
- Focus on maintenance and consultation campaigns
- Preserve budget for peak season

## Monitoring and Optimization

### Performance Triggers
- **Increase modifier**: If segment CPA 25% below campaign average
- **Decrease modifier**: If segment CPA 25% above campaign average
- **Remove modifier**: If segment shows no statistical difference

### Testing Protocol
- **A/B Test Changes**: 
- **Control for Variables**: Weather, seasonality, competitive activity
- **Scale Successful Changes**: Roll out proven adjustments across campaigns

## Emergency Scenarios

### Hurricane Watch/Warning
**Immediate Action**: Increase all bid modifiers by +100%
**Duration**: Warning period + 48 hours
**Rationale**: Emergency demand spike requires immediate response

### Competitive Pressure
**Detection**: Sudden CPC increase >50%
**Response**: Temporary bid increases to maintain market position
**Duration**: Until competitive pressure subsides

## Documentation Requirements

### Bid Adjustment Log
- Date of change
- Segment modified (device/geographic/audience)
- Modifier amount and direction
- Rationale and expected impact
- Performance results (post-change analysis)
