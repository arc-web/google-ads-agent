# Wright's Impact Window & Door - Strategic Ad Copy Best Practices Guide

## Overview

This comprehensive guide establishes the **strategic ad copy standards and best practices** for Wright's Impact Window & Door Google Ads campaigns. These are our internal optimization frameworks and technical writing standards.

**⚠️ IMPORTANT**: This document covers OUR strategy and standardization. For mandatory client-specific requirements that must be included in 100% of campaigns, see [`CLIENT_AD_COPY_REQUESTS.md`](CLIENT_AD_COPY_REQUESTS.md).

All strategic ad copy must follow these technical writing requirements while ensuring client requests are integrated intuitively.

## Character Limits Reference

### Google Ads Limits (as of 2026) - Technical Writing Standards
- **Headlines (RSA/PMAX)**: 30 characters maximum, **22-29 characters optimal** for value density
- **Descriptions (RSA/PMAX)**: 90 characters maximum, **75-85 characters optimal** for value density
- **Business Name (Gmail Ads)**: 20 characters maximum
- **Callout Extensions**: 25 characters per callout
- **Sitelink Text**: 25 characters
- **Sitelink Descriptions**: 35 characters

### Technical Writing Principles - Value Density Focus
- **Jam-Pack Value**: 22-29 chars headlines, 75-85 chars descriptions for maximum value density
- **Eliminate Filler Words**: No "the", "a", "an", "or", "and", "very", "really", "just", "that", "this"
- **No Em Dashes**: Avoid "—" and "–" - use hyphens or restructure for precision
- **Precision Language**: Every character must contribute to conversion or urgency
- **Value Density Scoring**: CTR evaluation prioritizes technical writing quality over readability
- **Character Efficiency**: Optimal ranges scientifically proven for maximum impact

## Campaign-Specific Ad Copy Guidelines

### Performance Max Campaigns
- **Headlines**: Mix of #1 positioning, brand recognition, and service excellence
- **Descriptions**: Value impact messaging with clear calls-to-action
- **Balance**: 40% service-focused, 30% regional, 20% brand, 10% #1 positioning

### Search Campaigns (RSA)
- **Headlines**: Match specific keyword themes and user intent
- **Descriptions**: Address pain points and include trust signals
- **Long-tail Keywords**: Include in descriptions for better relevance

### Local Service Campaigns
- **Headlines**: Heavy regional focus with city/county specificity
- **Descriptions**: Local service availability and emergency response
- **Trust Signals**: Local presence and community reputation
